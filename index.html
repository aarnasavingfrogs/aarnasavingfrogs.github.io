About Tree Frogs

Tree frogs are some of the most fascinating organisms on earth. These frogs have some of the craziest abilities and adaptations. Just a few of these are,
•Sticky pads on their feet to hang on trees or branches
•Ability to tolerate freezing temperatures by
•Amplifying their call by getting in groups
•Make their saliva sticky or thin to catch insects
These organisms have adapted to have amazing abilities to survive better in their environment

Tree frogs play an important role in their ecosystem. They eat insects like mosquitoes, beetles, etc. Without them, the ecosystem could become unbalanced and insect populations could become out of control.

However, these frogs are being wiped out of their environment all because of us.

So we need to take action immediately. We need to rebuild these environments that these frogs live in and protect them from extinction.

The Problem

Tree frogs are being wiped out of their environment — and it is all because of us.

Tree frog populations are decreasing at a rate of 3.79% each year. That number will only continue to rise as more forests are cut down for agriculture and urban development.

When forests are cut down, the tree frogs lose their homes, their food sources, and protection from predators. Many frogs also die from the illegal pet trade. Even though they are beautiful, they do not do well outside of their natural habitat.

Chytrid fungus is another serious threat. This deadly disease has already wiped out many frog populations worldwide. When the frogs' homes are destroyed, they become even more vulnerable to the fungus.

At this rate, many tree frog species could be extinct within decades. We need to act now.

How to Help

We need to take action immediately. We need to rebuild these environments that these frogs live in and protect them from extinction.

First, we will start by planting native plant and tree species in the area. Native species provide the food and shelter that tree frogs need to survive.

Then, we will remove any invasive species we have ever put in the area. This may take years to do, but it is necessary to restore the natural balance and give native wildlife a fighting chance.

Next, we will work to clean pollutants from the environment — pesticides, chemicals, and other contaminants that harm frog populations and their food sources.

Finally, we will try to pass an act to protect the areas from human destruction. We need legal protection to ensure these habitats are not destroyed again.

The goal is to give tree frog habitats enough time and space to heal so these frogs can keep living there for generations to come.
